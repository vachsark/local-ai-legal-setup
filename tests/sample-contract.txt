SOFTWARE SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Software Services Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of March 1, 2025 (the "Effective Date"), by and between:

Meridian Analytics, Inc., a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business at 450 Market Street, San Francisco, CA 94105 ("Company"), and

Brightwell Legal Solutions LLC, a California limited liability company with its principal place of business at 1200 Wilshire Blvd, Suite 800, Los Angeles, CA 90025 ("Client").

Company and Client are each referred to herein individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."

RECITALS

WHEREAS, Company desires to provide certain software services to Client; and

WHEREAS, Client desires to obtain such services from Company on the terms and conditions set forth herein.

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements set forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:


1. SERVICES

1.1 Scope of Services. Company shall provide Client with access to Company's proprietary legal document management platform (the "Platform") and associated support services as described in Exhibit A attached hereto (collectively, the "Services").

1.2 Service Level. Company shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure the Platform is available 99.5% of the time in any given calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance windows. Scheduled maintenance shall not exceed four (4) hours per month and shall occur between 2:00 a.m. and 6:00 a.m. Pacific Time.

1.3 Changes to Services. Company reserves the right to modify, update, or discontinue any feature of the Platform upon thirty (30) days' written notice to Client, provided that Company shall not materially reduce the core functionality of the Services during the Term without Client's prior written consent.


2. TERM AND TERMINATION

2.1 Initial Term. This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of two (2) years (the "Initial Term"), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Section 2.

2.2 Renewal. This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year periods (each, a "Renewal Term," and together with the Initial Term, the "Term") unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal to the other Party at least sixty (60) days prior to the expiration of the then-current Term.

2.3 Termination for Cause. Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail.

2.4 Termination for Convenience. Client may terminate this Agreement for any reason upon ninety (90) days' prior written notice to Company. Company may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon one hundred eighty (180) days' prior written notice to Client.

2.5 Effect of Termination. Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement: (a) all licenses granted herein shall immediately terminate; (b) Client shall promptly pay all outstanding fees; and (c) Company shall make Client's data available for export for a period of thirty (30) days following termination, after which Company may delete such data.

2.6 Survival. Sections 3 (Fees and Payment), 5 (Confidentiality), 6 (Intellectual Property), 7 (Indemnification), 8 (Limitation of Liability), and 9 (General) shall survive termination or expiration of this Agreement.


3. FEES AND PAYMENT

3.1 License Fee. Client shall pay Company an annual license fee of $84,000 (Eighty-Four Thousand Dollars), payable in quarterly installments of $21,000 (Twenty-One Thousand Dollars) each. The first installment is due on the Effective Date, and subsequent installments are due on the first day of each calendar quarter thereafter.

3.2 Implementation Fee. Client shall pay a one-time implementation fee of $15,000 (Fifteen Thousand Dollars) due within fifteen (15) days of the Effective Date.

3.3 Late Payment. Undisputed amounts not paid within thirty (30) days of the due date shall accrue interest at the rate of 1.5% per month (or the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, whichever is less).

3.4 Fee Increases. Company may increase fees upon renewal by no more than five percent (5%) above the prior Term's fees, provided Company provides written notice of any increase at least ninety (90) days before the start of the Renewal Term.

3.5 Taxes. All fees are exclusive of applicable taxes. Client is responsible for all sales, use, or similar taxes, excluding taxes based on Company's net income.

3.6 Disputed Invoices. Client must notify Company of any disputed invoice in writing within fifteen (15) days of receipt. Failure to timely dispute an invoice shall constitute acceptance.


4. CONFIDENTIALITY

4.1 Definition. "Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by one Party to the other that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure.

4.2 Obligations. Each Party shall: (a) hold the other Party's Confidential Information in strict confidence using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information, but no less than reasonable care; (b) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party except as permitted herein; and (c) use Confidential Information solely to fulfill its obligations or exercise its rights under this Agreement.

4.3 Exceptions. Confidentiality obligations do not apply to information that: (a) is or becomes publicly known through no breach of this Agreement; (b) was rightfully known before disclosure; (c) is independently developed without use of Confidential Information; or (d) is required to be disclosed by law or court order, provided the receiving Party gives prompt written notice to the disclosing Party.

4.4 Duration. Confidentiality obligations survive termination of this Agreement for a period of five (5) years.


5. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

5.1 Company IP. Company retains all right, title, and interest in and to the Platform, all underlying technology, and any improvements or derivatives thereof. This Agreement does not grant Client any ownership rights in the Platform.

5.2 Client Data. Client retains all right, title, and interest in and to all data submitted to the Platform by or on behalf of Client ("Client Data"). Client grants Company a limited, non-exclusive license to use Client Data solely to provide the Services.

5.3 Feedback. If Client provides any suggestions, ideas, or feedback regarding the Platform, Company may freely use such feedback without restriction or obligation to Client.

5.4 Work Product. Any custom development work specifically commissioned by Client and described in a written statement of work signed by both Parties shall be considered work made for hire, and ownership shall vest in Client upon full payment of all fees associated with such work.


6. INDEMNIFICATION

6.1 Company Indemnification. Company shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Client and its officers, directors, employees, and agents ("Client Indemnitees") from and against any third-party claims, damages, losses, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or relating to: (a) Company's infringement of any third-party intellectual property rights in connection with the Platform; or (b) Company's gross negligence or willful misconduct.

6.2 Client Indemnification. Client shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Company and its officers, directors, employees, and agents from and against any third-party claims arising out of or relating to: (a) Client's use of the Services in violation of this Agreement or applicable law; or (b) Client Data, including any claim that Client Data infringes any third-party right.

6.3 Indemnification Procedure. The indemnified Party shall: (a) promptly notify the indemnifying Party of any claim; (b) give the indemnifying Party sole control of the defense and settlement; and (c) provide reasonable cooperation. The indemnifying Party shall not settle any claim in a manner that imposes obligations on the indemnified Party without prior written consent.


7. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

7.1 Exclusion of Consequential Damages. IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, EXEMPLARY, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOST PROFITS, LOSS OF DATA, OR BUSINESS INTERRUPTION, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

7.2 Liability Cap. EACH PARTY'S TOTAL CUMULATIVE LIABILITY ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE TOTAL FEES PAID OR PAYABLE BY CLIENT IN THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING THE CLAIM.

7.3 Exceptions. The limitations in Sections 7.1 and 7.2 do not apply to: (a) a Party's indemnification obligations under Section 6; (b) a Party's breach of confidentiality obligations under Section 4; or (c) damages arising from gross negligence or willful misconduct.


8. NON-COMPETE AND NON-SOLICITATION

8.1 Non-Solicitation. During the Term and for a period of twelve (12) months following termination of this Agreement, neither Party shall, directly or indirectly, solicit or hire any employee of the other Party who was involved in the performance of this Agreement, without prior written consent.

8.2 No Non-Compete. Nothing in this Agreement restricts either Party from engaging in any business, including businesses that may compete with the other Party.


9. GENERAL

9.1 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

9.2 Dispute Resolution. Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be resolved by binding arbitration administered by JAMS under its Commercial Arbitration Rules. The arbitration shall take place in San Francisco, California. Judgment on the award may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction. EACH PARTY WAIVES ITS RIGHT TO A JURY TRIAL.

9.3 Notices. All notices must be in writing and delivered by: (a) personal delivery; (b) certified mail, return receipt requested; or (c) nationally recognized overnight courier. Notices are effective upon receipt.

9.4 Force Majeure. Neither Party shall be liable for any failure or delay in performance due to causes beyond its reasonable control, including acts of God, war, terrorism, pandemic, or government action, provided the affected Party provides prompt written notice and uses reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact.

9.5 Entire Agreement. This Agreement, together with all Exhibits, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to its subject matter and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, warranties, and understandings.

9.6 Amendment. This Agreement may only be amended by a written instrument signed by authorized representatives of both Parties.

9.7 Waiver. No waiver of any provision shall be effective unless in writing and signed by the waiving Party.

9.8 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

9.9 Assignment. Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other Party, except that Company may assign this Agreement in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or substantially all of its assets without Client's consent, provided Company provides written notice within thirty (30) days of such assignment.

9.10 Counterparts. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original.


EXHIBIT A — SERVICES DESCRIPTION

The Services include:
1. Access to the Meridian Analytics Platform for up to fifty (50) named user accounts.
2. Standard technical support via email and chat during business hours (Monday-Friday, 9:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. Pacific Time), with response times of four (4) business hours for critical issues and two (2) business days for non-critical issues.
3. Automated document processing up to 10,000 pages per month. Overage is billed at $0.02 per page.
4. Up to 500 GB of secure cloud storage. Additional storage is available at $50 per 100 GB per month.
5. Standard data export in PDF and CSV formats.
6. Security updates and patches as released.
